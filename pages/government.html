CITY COUNCIL

Election Information

Council Members serve four year terms:
  • The Primary Election is held on the 2nd Tuesday of March
  • The General Election is held on the 2nd Tuesday of May
  • Special Elections may be held as needed
  • Elections are held during odd number years with 3 or 4 seats being filled each election (4 seats in 2007, 3 seats in 2009)
  • Following a regular city election, the council shall meet on the following Monday for the purpose of choosing a mayor from among its membership, appointing the regular appointed officers, and transacting such other business as they deem necessary.

CITY DEPARTMENTS

The City Clerk is the Custodian of all formal city documents.

These duties include:

  • Performing duties as may be required by law or ordinance.
  • Maintaining seal and attest documents.
  • Maintaining other documents such as agendas, council meeting minutes, ordinances, proclamations, resolutions and documents as required by Mayor and Council.
  • Keeping records of licenses and permits

The City Attorney is appointed by the City Council and serves as legal advisor to the City Council, City Manager, and all of the City’s departments regarding a variety of municipal law issues. The City Attorney reviews all ordinances, resolutions, contracts, and other legal documents involving the City. The City Attorney also defends the City and pursues legal claims on its behalf either directly or through outside legal counsel which he supervises. The City Attorney is committed to providing the highest quality legal representation possible to meet the present and future needs of the City in an efficient and effective manner, with protecting the City, its residents, and its traditions by minimizing the City's legal liabilities.

The City Attorney also prosecutes misdemeanor crimes in violation of the Arizona Revised Statutes and the South Tucson City Code occurring within the City limits, including Driving Under the Influence (DUI), Shoplifting, Assault, Domestic Violence, minor drug offenses, traffic offenses and other misdemeanor offenses, in South Tucson Municipal Court. The City Attorney works with the South Tucson Police Department and other City departments in the investigation, charging, and prosecution of criminal cases within City limits to diligently prosecute those who violate the legal protections that protect the City and its residents. The City Attorney also safeguards the constitutional rights of crime victims and criminal defendants while keeping crime victims informed of the progress of their cases and allowing them meaningful input regarding the resolution of their cases.
